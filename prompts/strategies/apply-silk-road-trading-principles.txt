For centuries, the Silk Road facilitated exchange between civilizations despite vast distances, language barriers, and cultural differences.

*   What valuable commodities (ideas, resources, skills) do you have to trade, and what do you seek from distant 'kingdoms'?
*   How can you establish trustworthy intermediaries or caravanserai (safe trading posts) to facilitate exchange across boundaries?
*   What cultural translation is needed to make your offerings valuable in different contexts?

Consider how establishing durable trade routes across organizational, disciplinary, or cultural barriers might enrich your work through exposure to radically different perspectives and resources. What would be your silk, spices, or porcelain?
