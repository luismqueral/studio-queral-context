Medieval guilds combined quality control, knowledge transfer, and market regulation through a structured system of apprentices, journeymen, and masters.

*   How might clearly defined stages of expertise development improve quality in your context?
*   What tacit knowledge needs to be preserved and transmitted through direct mentorship rather than formal documentation?
*   How could you create both collaboration and healthy competition within a community of practice?

Consider how incorporating elements of the guild system—shared standards, skill progression, collective reputation, and mutual support—might foster excellence and innovation while maintaining tradition. What would be your masterpiece?
