Psychogeography explores how the physical environment (streets, architecture, landscapes) unconsciously affects our emotions and behavior.

Consider the 'emotional landscape' or 'psychological terrain' of this situation, even if it's not tied to a physical place.

*   What are the 'desire paths,' 'hidden alleys,' or 'intimidating structures' in its conceptual space?
*   How do these 'environmental' factors influence how people navigate or feel about the situation?
*   Could 'redesigning' aspects of this conceptual environment alter engagement or outcomes?

Map this psychogeography and its subtle influences.
