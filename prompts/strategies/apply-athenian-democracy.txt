Ancient Athens pioneered direct democracy, where citizens collectively deliberated and voted on important matters.

*   What would happen if your challenge were subject to an Athenian-style assembly?
*   How might broader participation, public deliberation, and collective decision-making transform your approach?
*   What wisdom might emerge from the demos that expert opinion might miss?

Explore how creating a temporary agora—a public space for open exchange—might generate more legitimate and creative solutions than top-down approaches. But also consider the limitations: who would be excluded from your 'citizenship,' and what checks might prevent mob rule?
