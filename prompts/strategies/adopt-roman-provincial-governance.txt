The Roman Empire maintained control over diverse territories through a sophisticated balance of central authority and local autonomy.

*   How might you apply the principle of 'divide et impera' (divide and rule) to break down complex challenges into manageable regions or domains?
*   When should you impose uniform standards (like Roman law), and when should you accommodate local customs and practices?
*   What infrastructure—physical or conceptual—could you build to bind disparate elements together?

Consider how strategic delegation with clear reporting structures might allow you to extend your influence far beyond what you could directly control. What would your version of Roman roads, aqueducts, and coinage be?
